TL;DR A compact 0.04B feed-forward model that takes a calibrated mix of fisheye and pinhole views and predicts dense metric depth plus a global scale in one pass, running in real time on six-view heterogeneous rigs.

Abstract

One compact model, fisheye and pinhole, one metric space.

We present X-Lens, a compact feed-forward model for metric depth estimation from a variable number of calibrated fisheye and pinhole views. To meet real-time downstream demands, X-Lens adopts a "simple yet effective" design guided by two core principles. First, two lightweight modules handle heterogeneous cameras: learnable calibration tokens align fisheye and pinhole projective spaces, and a Jacobian-parameterized distortion bias in cross-attention enforces cross-camera consistency, letting a 0.04B model generalize robustly. Second, X-Lens predicts only depth and a global scale, a minimalist objective that eases convergence.

To supervise this design, we train X-Lens on multiple public datasets and OmniScene, our newly released large-scale synthetic dataset comprising approximately 266K synchronized six-view frames across 103 indoor and outdoor scenes. Without benchmark-specific fine-tuning, X-Lens attains the best reported results on the heterogeneous OmniScene-Full benchmark, lowering metric depth AbsRel by 25.4% over the strongest baseline while using 88.9% fewer parameters, and it remains competitive on homogeneous fisheye-only and pinhole-only settings. Furthermore, we integrate X-Lens as a geometry-aware visual encoder into a Vision-Action model, demonstrating its utility for multi-view robotic manipulation.

OmniScene Dataset

A large-scale synthetic dataset for multi-view metric depth with heterogeneous cameras, released openly on Hugging Face.

103complex scenes
564motion sequences
266Ksix-view frames
1.7M+calibrated images

Scenes span residential, commercial, industrial, urban, and sci-fi environments built from professionally authored Kujiale and Unreal Engine assets. Every frame carries dense, noise-free metric ground truth: pixel-accurate z-depth, validity masks, sky indicators, and per-frame OpenCV-convention intrinsics and extrinsics. Validation is drawn from the training scenes, while the test set is held out on fully disjoint scenes for evaluation on unseen environments.
