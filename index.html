TL;DR A single feed-forward transformer that ingests images from any mix of cameras — wide-angle, fisheye, telephoto, even rolling-shutter event sensors — and recovers a metric, globally-consistent 3D space with depth, pose, and dense geometry in one pass.

Abstract

One model, any camera rig, one coherent space.

Modern robots and capture stages mix sensors with wildly different optics, resolutions, and shutters. Prior pipelines treat each modality separately and stitch results with brittle calibration. UniMVS replaces that stack with a single geometry-grounded transformer. Trained on 14M heterogeneous frames, it predicts a shared latent space token that decodes simultaneously into depth, camera pose, and a dense metric point map — no per-rig calibration required.

Across eight benchmarks, UniMVS improves point-map accuracy by 34.7% and pose estimation by 29.1% over the strongest baselines, while running 2.6× faster. The same checkpoint generalizes from drone swarms to surgical endoscopes without fine-tuning.

Abilities

Four capabilities, zero retraining.

01

Video Reconstruction

Turn an unposed monocular clip into a temporally stable, metric 3D scan. UniMVS tracks geometry frame-to-frame and fuses it into a single drift-free mesh.

02

SLAM for Large-Scale Scenes

Drop-in front-end for kilometer-scale mapping. Loop closures emerge from the shared latent space, so global consistency holds even across day–night and sensor changes.

03

Feed-Forward 3D Gaussians

Predict a full 3D Gaussian splat in a single forward pass — no per-scene optimization. Render novel views at 120 FPS straight from heterogeneous inputs.

04

Spatial Perception, Many Cameras

Fuse a rig of mismatched sensors — fisheye, tele, thermal — into one calibrated metric world. Auto-resolves extrinsics on the fly, even as cameras are added or removed.
